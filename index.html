Water Quality Prediction

The project aims to develop a machine learning model to predict the potability of water based on various water quality parameters. The model will be trained on a dataset containing measurements of various parameters such as pH, hardness, solids, chloramines, sulfate, organic carbon, trihalomethanes, turbidity, and conductivity, as well as information on whether the water is potable or not.

Shared Mobility: Predicting Bike Share Demand

Bike sharing can be described as “a pool of publicly available bicycles placed around the city and ready to be used for a low payment.” However, the issue with this systems is the inability to accurately know the demand at a given station at a given time. Thus, this research was aimed at solving this problem by deploying a ML model (random forest regressor) in the prediction of the bike share demand in New York City.

Detecting Parkinson Disease Using XGBoost

The use of data science in healthcare has the potential to improve services and outcomes, particularly when it comes to early detection of diseases. In this project, we will use Python to build a system for detecting Parkinson's Disease, a degenerative disorder of the nervous system that impacts movement and can cause tremors and stiffness. By using data science to identify this condition early on, we can potentially improve the prognosis for those affected.
